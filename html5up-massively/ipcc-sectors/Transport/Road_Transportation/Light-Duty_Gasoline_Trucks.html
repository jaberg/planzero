Transport/
Road Transportation
Light Duty Gasoline Trucks

Emissions from the operation of light-duty gasoline trucks is Canada's third-largest emissions sector. While engine efficiency continues to improve, Canada's population continues to grow, and larger vehicles such as SUVs have become more popular. Crossover SUVs, SUVs, and minivans are included in this emissions sector. The technology of hybrid and EV alternatives to internal combustion engines seems to be superior, and is currently crossing the chasm to mainstream adoption.

Causes of Emissions

This sector measures emissions from the combustion of gasoline from vehicles such as
  • SUVs / Crossovers: (e.g., Ford Explorer, Toyota RAV4, Honda CR-V)
  • Minivans: (e.g., Dodge Caravan, Honda Odyssey)
  • Pickup Trucks: (e.g., Ford F-150, Chevy Silverado)
  • Commercial Vans: (e.g., Ford Transit Connect)
Definition of this class of vehicles: Vehicles with a GVWR of less than 3,900 kg (8,500 lbs) that are designed for off-road operation, or have a certain cargo capacity that places them above light-duty gasoline vehicles, which is a separate IPCC emissions sector focused on sedans, hatchbacks, and coupes.
