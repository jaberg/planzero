Summary

Vessel assumptions:

  • Vessel can charge / battery-swap at most once per day for 1-2 hours.
  • Vessel requires a sustained {{ project.power_required_per_working_day }} under steady steaming, which can usually maintain 12 knots
  • Battery capacity: {{project.battery_capacity.to('megawatt hours')}}
  • Manufacturing budget for battery: {{ project.battery_cost.to(u.megaCAD) }}
  • Manufacturing budget for remainder of vessel: {{ project.non_battery_cost.to(u.megaCAD) }}
  • Vessel accommodates 18 crew.
  • Vessel can be remote-operated to develop piloting AI, unlock future lower-cost tugs without crew quarters.
  • Vessel capacity is {{ project.deadweight }} (up to {{ project.pre_battery_deadweight }}, except battery weight {{project.battery_mass.to('tonne')}})

Charging assumptions:

  • The vessels can be charged for an amortized cost of $0.10/kwh, with negligible emissions.
  • Electricity for charging to be obtained by grid power, wind turbine, or solar arrays on piers or barges, with diesel genset backup.
  • Unclear whether battery would be swapped mechanically or recharged via cable.
